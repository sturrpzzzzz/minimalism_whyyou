I used to wonder why other people were so privileged.

No matter how much I tried, there is always someone prettier.
Smarter.
Richer.

There was never a moment in which I was satisfied with myself. I would better myself, just to see someone better than me.
So why does it matter?

It was so easy to just keep dragging myself down and feel miserable. That's why I was completely offended when someone told me I could just... choose to be happy with myself. What do you mean I can fix a problem I have had my whole life just by... changing my mind? What do you mean I can just choose not to be sad, as if I was causing my misery instead of the world?

It wasn't easy to change the way I have been thinking my whole life, but I forced myself to see the good in everything no matter what, because I was sick of living under the rain everyday. What do I have to lose from thinking positively anyway?

Now, I am happier and more grateful for my life than ever, and I feel better about myself than ever.

And do you know what the best thing about all this is?

We, as a society, have normalised and encouraged negativity to the point that we have rewired our brains to automatically find and focus on something to complain about.
It is infinitely easier to spiral into negativity than to do something about it. There is always something good coming out of any situation, something to be happy about in our lives no matter how big or small, but seeing it is a choice.

And remember, you are always infinitely more beautiful and capable than you think you are.

It is scientifically proven that repeating a behaviour for at least 10000 times rewires your brain and develops new habits.
No matter how heavily perpetuated negativity is in your lives, if you keep choosing to plant the garden of your mind with positive thoughts, your mind will automatically come to positive conclusions.

We see the world as we interpret it. Our interpretation comes from what we focus on. The key is to see the positive in everything first.

Whether or not you experience happiness is within your control.

So what will you choose?
